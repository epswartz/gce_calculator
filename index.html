Under New York State's Good Cause Eviction law, landlords in NYC and other opted-in municipalities cannot evict covered tenants for not paying rent if the nonpayment is due to an "unreasonable rent increase." The threshold for an unreasonable increase is based on the inflation for the previous calendar year at the time of the increase.
